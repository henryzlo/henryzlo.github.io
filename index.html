About Me


My name is Henry Z. Lo. I am:
  • a (graduating) PhD student at UMass Boston studying machine learning, specifically neural networks (deep learning) and optimization.
  • a full-stack web developer, with work experience in Python, PHP, etc.
  • an occasional instructor and lecturer
  • co-founder, with Joseph Paul Cohen of the Institute for Reproducible Research, a (pending) nonprofit with several online projects

My dissertation was Deep Networks: Applications, Interpretability and Optimization, which consisted of my work in crater detection, the FG paper, and an ICLR workshop paper.

My master's thesis was Querying Deep Neural Networks with Gradients, which was based on my FG paper.

Some first-author papers:
  • Scale Normalization, ICLR Workshop 2016
  • Prediction Gradients for Feature Extraction and Analysis from Convolutional Neural Networks, FG 2015
  • Temporality and Context for Detecting Adverse Drug Reactions from Longitudinal Data, Applied Intelligence 2014
